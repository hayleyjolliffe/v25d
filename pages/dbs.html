DBS

Reworking and scaling internal systems to reduce bottlenecks and support a season of rapid growth.

Context

Brought on initially as a Digital Designer with leadership buy-in, I identified critical bottlenecks in DBS's internal operations and expanded my role into system design and design ops.

Problem

The agency's workflows, file systems, and SOPs had not scaled beyond a 1â€“3 person team. As client volume and team size grew, inefficiencies multiplied.

Outdated templates, inconsistent access, and fractured information flow caused frequent errors and delays.

Challenge

Initially tasked with updating templates, I realized the problem was more complex than that. I proposed and led a full systems overhaul, reframing the challenge as a design ops initiative.

My work expanded to answer the high level question:

"How might we scale an internal design system to support rapid company and client growth without adding workflow bottlenecks?"

Strategy

To answer this new question, my focus shifted.

The goal was to eliminate redundancies, streamline access, and create scalable systems that empowered the entire team.

Approach

I started by conducting audits of all workflows, tools, and templates, while tracking bottlenecks in live client work.

With bottlenecks tracked, I was able to map redundancies, build updated file standards, and rebuild key systems in the tools most used by the team.

I centralized instructions and SOPs into one documentation hub, testing frequently with non-technical teammates to ensure clarity and deploy without need for additional training.

I owned the project end-to-end, from audit through rollout.

Impact

The overhaul drastically reduced errors, cut handoff delays, and enabled smoother onboarding for both clients and team members.

The new system improved daily efficiency and was praised by leadership as a foundation for continued scaling.
