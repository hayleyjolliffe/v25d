ENPOWR

Scalable design system for non-technical stakeholders, balancing youth appeal with government constraints.

In collaboration with DBS.

ENPOWR Website  |  @enpowr_endofound

Context

At DBS, I partnered with the Endometriosis Foundation of America on ENPOWR, a nationwide youth education initiative.

Initially brought in as a brand designer, my role expanded to focus on systems design, where I had full ownership of creating scalable brand and campaign frameworks.

Problem

ENPOWR's mission was to deliver comprehensive menstrual health education and reduce the average 10-year delay to endometriosis diagnosis.

The challenge was twofold:

  1. Design educational materials that resonate with high school students, while operating within the constraints of a government-affiliated nonprofit.
  2. Equip non-technical stakeholders with the tools to independently create campaigns and collateral without relying on designers.

Challenge

With the team tackling the project's identity, I had a different question to answer:

“How might we design a flexible system that educates and empowers high school students while being simple enough for non-technical stakeholders to run independently?”

Strategy

To answer both high level questions, I developed a dual-layered design system that incorporated the identity the team developed:

  1. A flexible, youth-appropriate visual identity tailored to resonate with a high school audience.
  2. A practical toolkit of ready-to-use templates and components, built around tools stakeholders already knew (incl. Canva and Google Workspace), drastically reducing the technical barrier to execution.

Approach

I led with researching comparable government and nonprofit education initiatives to analyze how they balanced approachability with institutional credibility.

When the stakeholders' technical gap was identified, I reframed the design solution around accessibility of use, not just visual consistency.

As the team developed the identity within our workflows, I rebuilt critical touchpoints in the same tools EndoFound used, eventually leading creation of the systems that could be stress-tested directly with the stakeholders.

Impact

While metrics were not shared due to NDA, stakeholders reported significant improvements in workflow efficiency.

By removing barriers around collateral creation, the system freed their time and focus for the initiative's core mission: educating students and fostering self-advocacy.
